Chronicle of Passages

1888

The Genesis

In the twilight of the Victorian era, Dorabjee Naorojee Mithaiwala, a Parsee merchant of vision, establishes the Kowloon Ferry Company. Four humble vessels begin their eternal dance across the harbour, connecting two worlds separated by water yet bound by destiny.

1898

Birth of the Stars

The company transforms, adopting a celestial nomenclature. Each vessel becomes a star ‚Äî Morning Star, Evening Star, Rising Star, Guiding Star ‚Äî navigating not just waters but the firmament of Hong Kong's collective imagination.

1916

The Golden Age Begins

Electric lights illuminate the vessels for the first time, transforming the harbour into a constellation of moving lights. The ferry becomes not merely transport but spectacle, a floating theater of modernity.

1941

Dark Waters

War descends. Japanese forces commandeer the fleet. The Golden Star and Meridian Star are lost to enemy action. Yet through occupation and hardship, skeletal service continues ‚Äî a lifeline that refuses to be severed.

1966

Five Cents of Fury

A mere five-cent fare increase ignites the powder keg of social tension. The Star Ferry riots mark a watershed in Hong Kong's social consciousness. The ferry, symbol of daily life, becomes the stage for historical transformation.

1978

Renaissance

Air-conditioning graces the upper decks. Modern vessels join the fleet while maintaining the characteristic green and white livery. Progress embraces tradition in a delicate waltz of preservation and innovation.

2006

The Last Crossing

Edinburgh Place pier closes after 49 years. The Queen's Pier follows. Thousands gather for final crossings, bearing witness to the end of an epoch. The clock tower's final chime echoes across waters heavy with memory.

Today

Eternal Voyage

136 years of continuous service. 70,000 daily passages. The Star Ferry endures ‚Äî not as relic but as living heritage, each crossing a thread in the tapestry of Hong Kong's soul. The stars still sail, and the harbour remembers.
