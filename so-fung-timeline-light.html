An Eventful Life

1937

The Genesis

In the shipyards of Causeway Bay, the Ah King Slipway gives birth to elegance. Designed by the legendary Sparkman & Stephens, a 25-meter gaff-rigged schooner emerges from timber and dreams. So Fong ‚Äî a vessel destined for adventure, crafted with the precision of naval architecture and the soul of Hong Kong.

1941

Service in War

War transforms the elegant schooner into a vessel of duty. So Fong answers the call, serving with the United States Coast Guard during the darkest hours of the Pacific conflict. The yacht becomes a guardian, trading leisure for service, elegance for purpose.

1970s

The Long Captivity

The Cold War casts its shadow. So Fong falls into the hands of the Vietnamese government, held captive for over a decade. The elegant schooner becomes a prisoner of politics, its sails stilled, its spirit waiting. Yet even in captivity, the vessel endures, a testament to resilience.

1941

Duty in Darkness

The Pacific War rages. So Fong transforms from pleasure yacht to guardian of the seas, serving with the United States Coast Guard. Through the darkest hours, the elegant schooner navigates conflict, proving that beauty and purpose can sail together.

1980s

Liberation

After more than a decade of captivity, So Fong is freed. The schooner emerges from its long imprisonment, ready to sail again. Multiple refits restore its elegance, each restoration a chapter in the vessel's remarkable resilience. The yacht prepares for new adventures across the world's oceans.

1990s

Racing Glory

So Fong returns to the world stage, competing in classic yacht regattas across the Mediterranean and beyond. The restored schooner proves that age and elegance can triumph, its gaff-rigged sails catching the wind with the grace of a vessel reborn. Each race writes a new page in an already storied history.

2006

Triumph at St. Tropez

In the azure waters of the French Riviera, So Fong claims victory. The Rolex Voiles de St. Tropez ‚Äî a crown jewel among classic yacht regattas. After decades of war, captivity, and restoration, the schooner proves that perseverance and elegance can conquer the most prestigious waters.

Today

Return to Origins

After nearly nine decades of adventure, So Fong returns home. Sold to an American businessman residing in Hong Kong, the schooner sails back to the waters where it was born. Now part of the fleet managed by Hong Kong Historic Vessels Ltd, So Fong continues its eventful life ‚Äî a living legend, a testament to maritime heritage.
